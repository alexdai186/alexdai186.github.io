What's my tip-of-the-week?

My tip of the week is to use Leetcode to practice coing interview problems. It's got a great library of problems that cover all of the possible topics companies can ask you, from binary search trees to linked lists to dynamic programming. In addition, it's got company specific questions, that is, questions that are closely linked to certain companies. Amazon might ask different types of question at a different frequency than Facebook, and so it really helps to make your studying more efficient and directed towards the company of choice.
The final advantage of using Leetcode over other competitors like Hackerrank is that it handles all of the input processing for you. One of the biggest annoyances I have with Hackerrank is that I probably spend 5-10 minutes per problem just mapping out the input parsing logic, something that is both redundant and useless for interviewing. Leetcode just has you implement a method, making its user experience much more enjoyable and streamlined. Check it out!
